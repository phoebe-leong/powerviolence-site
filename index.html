Powerviolence

What is powerviolence

Powerviolence is an extreme subgenre of hardcore punk, emphasising rawness, speed, abrupt tempo changes, and short songs.
It was most popular in the 1990s. Recently the genre has seen a rise in popularity again.

Bands / Albums

In the 90s, there were a few influential, popular bands. Namely Siege (the band that started it all), Spazz, Capitalist Casualties, among others.



In 1984, Siege's demo was released. It sounded like nothing else in hardcore at that time. It wasn't crossover, it wasn't normal hardcore, it wasn't grindcore.
Their demo was the only music they released, but they started a whole new movement; what is now known as powerviolence.

Spazz is also a well known powerviolence band, known for their weird and nerdy humour, samples from kung-fu films and B movies, and occasional use of hip-hop beats
as well as absurdly long song titles (e.g., "A Prayer For Complete And Utter Eradication of All Generic Pop-Punk"). Most likely due to the recent resurgence in powerviolence popularity, Tankcrimes has re-released Spazz's discography.

With the new surge in popularity of powerviolence, of course came new powerviolence bands.

Labels

In California in the 1990s, powerviolence was centralised around Slap-a-Ham Records who signed bands such as Man Is The Bastard, Capitalist Casualties, Spazz, etc.

  • Slap-a-Ham Records (1989-2002)
  • 625 Thrashcore (1993-present)
